The Photographers

Katerina Apostolidi

Katerina Apostolidi is a photographer based in Athens, Greece. Her work explores themes of memory, place, and the passage of time through intimate observations of everyday life.

With a background in visual arts, Katerina's photographic practice focuses on capturing the subtle poetry found in ordinary moments. Her images invite viewers to slow down and contemplate the relationships between people, spaces, and memories.

Her work has been exhibited in galleries across Europe and published in various photography journals and magazines.

Kostas Tsanakas

Kostas Tsanakas is a documentary photographer whose work examines the intersection of landscape, culture, and personal narrative. Based in Greece, his photographs reflect a deep engagement with the environments he encounters.

Through his lens, Kostas captures the essence of places and the stories they hold, creating visual narratives that speak to both the universal and the particular aspects of human experience.

His collaborative projects have been featured in international exhibitions, and his work continues to explore the ways photography can preserve and reimagine collective memory.

The Curator

Danae Nagel

Danae Nagel is an independent curator and art critic specializing in contemporary photography and visual culture. With extensive experience in curating exhibitions that explore the relationship between image-making and collective memory, she brings a thoughtful perspective to photographic narratives.

Her curatorial practice is characterized by a deep interest in how photography mediates our understanding of place, identity, and time. She has worked with numerous galleries and cultural institutions, developing exhibitions that create meaningful dialogues between artists and audiences.

Through her work, Danae seeks to create spaces where photography can be experienced not just as documentation, but as a medium for reflection, conversation, and connection.

Note From The Curator

Danae Nagel

In the photographs of Katerina Apostolidi and Kostas Tsanakas, movement and stillness collide. People in transit, waiting, working. Fragments of a constantly shifting present. A glance, a hand, a gesture – familiar and fleeting at the same time.

'On the Memory of Travel' explores travel as a state, not as a destination. The images speak of movement, memory, loss – of bodies on the move, of places in flux, of shifting borders. Movement here appears not as an exception, but as a shared experience. From between ports, streets and railway stations, a silent archive of the in-between emerges – the moment just before something is about to begin or to end.

The camera does not observe from the outside. It accompanies, listens and reacts. Its images speak not of possession, but of presence – the assertion that closeness remains possible. In every image lies a residue of light, of time, of memory. Living together is not a promise – it arises from action and attentiveness. There is a light that never goes out.

— Danae Nagel, November 2025

Myxa Cafe

About Myxa

Myxa is a cozy Greek-Mediterranean café-bistro located in the heart of Neukölln, Berlin. Since 2011, this charming corner café with its two big windows has been more than just a place to eat and drink—it's become a cultural hub where art, community, and authentic Greek flavors come together.

The café is known for its homemade Greek-Mediterranean inspired food using genuine ingredients from Crete, including extra virgin olive oil and hand-picked teas and herbs. With its beautiful 1920s Berlin-style interior, handmade furniture, and wooden floors, Myxa offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

Beyond serving delicious food and excellent coffee from KUDU Coffee Roasters, Myxa regularly hosts art exhibitions, live music events, and cultural gatherings, making it the perfect venue for "On the Memory of Travel."
